# Mathematical Physics · Interactive

**The mathematical methods every physicist needs**

**Status:** Planned

Part of [Hengzhe's Science Classroom](https://hengzhe-science-classroom.github.io/hub/) · Tier 4 · Graduate

## What You'll Learn

- Vector analysis and curvilinear coordinates
- Matrices, tensors, and group theory
- Complex analysis for physics
- Differential equations and Green's functions
- Special functions: Bessel, Legendre, spherical harmonics
- Fourier and Laplace transforms
- Variational methods

## References

- George B. Arfken, Hans J. Weber & Frank E. Harris, *Mathematical Methods for Physicists* (7th ed., Academic Press)
- Sadri Hassani, *Mathematical Physics* (2nd ed., Springer)
- Mary L. Boas, *Mathematical Methods in the Physical Sciences* (3rd ed., Wiley)

## Features (when built)

- **Interactive visualizations** — every concept comes alive with draggable, animated demos
- **KaTeX math rendering** — rigorous notation throughout
- **Exercises with hints & solutions** — from computational to proof-based
- **Progress tracking** — saved locally in your browser
- **Zero dependencies** — just open the link and learn
